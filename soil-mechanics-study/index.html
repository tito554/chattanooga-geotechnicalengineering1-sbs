Technical details of the service in Chattanooga

We start with a site reconnaissance to map out visible features like sinkholes or springs common in Chattanooga's limestone bedrock. Then we drill boreholes using hollow-stem augers, retrieving undisturbed samples for lab testing. The equipment we bring includes a track-mounted drill rig for steep slopes and a portable unit for tight urban lots. In the lab, we run triaxial compression tests (ASTM D4767-11) and consolidation tests to determine settlement parameters. For projects on the Southside or near the river, we also perform MASW-Vs30 surveys to map soil stiffness profiles without drilling. The key parameters we focus on are the angle of internal friction, cohesion, and the coefficient of volume compressibility.

Local geotechnical conditions in Chattanooga

Chattanooga expanded rapidly in the 20th century, filling low-lying areas with imported fill and building over limestone caves. A soil mechanics study is critical here because the risk of differential settlement is real. We have seen cases where a building's corner sank 10 cm due to an undetected void below the slab. The Tennessee Valley Authority flood control projects also altered groundwater levels, which can trigger sinkhole collapse. Our studies include a collapse potential assessment and a groundwater monitoring plan to flag these hazards early.

Our services

We offer three core services for soil mechanics studies in Chattanooga, each tailored to the local geology and project type.

Site Investigation & Sampling

We drill boreholes up to 30 meters deep using hollow-stem augers or rock coring. Samples are extracted with split-spoon samplers for SPT and thin-walled tubes for undisturbed clay samples. Each hole is logged by a geotechnical engineer.

Laboratory Testing Suite

Our in-house lab runs index tests (Atterberg limits, grain size) and strength tests (triaxial, direct shear, unconfined compression). For settlement analysis, we perform one-dimensional consolidation tests on Chatanooga clays.

Geotechnical Report & Recommendations

We compile all field and lab data into a comprehensive report. It includes soil profiles, bearing capacity calculations, settlement estimates, and foundation recommendations per IBC 2021. The report is stamped by a licensed professional engineer.

Common questions

How long does a soil mechanics study take in Chattanooga?

A typical study takes 2 to 4 weeks from mobilization to final report. Drilling and sampling take 2 to 5 days depending on the number of borings and site access restrictions. Lab testing adds another 7 to 14 days.

What is the cost range for a soil mechanics study in Chattanooga?

For a standard residential or small commercial project, the cost ranges between US$3,530 and US$4,630. This includes drilling up to three boreholes, full lab testing, and a geotechnical report. Larger or more complex sites may cost more.

Do I need a soil mechanics study for a small addition or deck?

In Chattanooga, even small projects benefit from a study if the soil is suspect. For a deck or light addition, a limited investigation with two hand auger borings and basic lab tests may suffice. We can scope it to fit your budget.

What makes Chattanooga's soil different from other cities?

The primary difference is the karst geology. Limestone bedrock dissolves over time, creating voids and sinkholes. Also, the river terraces contain layers of soft clay and loose sand that settle unevenly. A standard study must address these specific risks.
