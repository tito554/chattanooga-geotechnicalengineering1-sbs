Chattanooga sits on a mix of river deposits from the Tennessee River and residual soils from weathered limestone of the Cumberland Plateau. Many projects here encounter variable fill layers over stiff clays or shallow rock. Before we finalize foundation recommendations, we run the SPT (Standard Penetration Test) to get N-values at 1.5-meter intervals. Those numbers directly feed into bearing capacity and settlement calculations. For shallow foundations on clay, we often pair SPT with a placa de carga to validate modulus of subgrade reaction. On tighter urban lots near downtown, we coordinate with micropilotes when access is limited but loads are high.

SPT N-values in Chattanooga typically range from 8 in soft clay to over 50 in dense sand or weathered limestone.

Technical details of the service in Chattanooga

We use a standard 63.5-kg automatic trip hammer with a 76-cm drop height, per ASTM D1586-18. The split-spoon sampler is driven 45 cm, and we record blow counts for each 15-cm increment. The sum of the last two increments gives the N-value. Our rigs handle depths up to 30 meters in Chattanooga, but typical boreholes stop around 15 m due to rock or dense sand. Key parameters recorded:
  • N-value (blows per 30 cm)
  • Soil type and color (USCS classification)
  • Moisture content and consistency
  • Depth to groundwater
We also log drilling resistance and note any cobbles or boulders. For fine-grained layers, we retrieve undisturbed tube samples for lab testing like limites-atterberg and consolidation.

Local geotechnical conditions in Chattanooga

The main geotechnical risk in Chattanooga is encountering karst features — solution cavities and pinnacles in the limestone bedrock. SPT alone may not detect a void if the spoon hits a rock ledge. For this reason, we recommend supplementing SPT with a georradar-gpr survey on sites with known sinkhole activity. Another risk is underestimating settlement in soft alluvial clays along the river; N-values below 4 in those layers require careful analysis and often ground improvement. Our field team flags any sudden drops in blow count immediately.

Common questions

How deep should SPT boreholes be for a typical home in Chattanooga?

For a two-story residence on slab-on-grade, we recommend SPT boreholes to at least 6 m, or until refusal on rock. Deeper holes (10-15 m) are needed if the design includes a basement or if fill layers exceed 3 m.

What does an N-value of 10 mean for foundation design?

An SPT N-value of 10 indicates loose to medium-dense sand or firm clay. For shallow footings, allowable bearing capacity typically ranges from 100 to 150 kPa. Settlement analysis is recommended, especially if groundwater is near the surface.

Can SPT detect voids or cavities in limestone?

SPT can hint at voids if the blow count drops suddenly (e.g., from 50 to 2), but it is not reliable for cavity mapping. For karst-prone areas in Chattanooga, we recommend supplementing with GPR or microtremor testing.

How much does an SPT test cost in Chattanooga?

A standard SPT borehole (10 m depth, including mobilization and logging) typically ranges from US$600 to US$830. Final cost depends on site access, number of boreholes, and whether lab testing is included.
